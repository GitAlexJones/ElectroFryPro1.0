Electrofishing Theory

1. Ohm's Law (V = I × R)

Voltage (V) = Current (I) × Resistance (R). Water conductivity determines resistance. Low conductivity = high resistance (requires higher voltage). High conductivity = low resistance (requires lower voltage).

2. Power Transfer Theory

Peak efficiency occurs when water conductivity matches the fish's internal conductivity (~100-150 µS/cm). In highly conductive water, electricity flows around the fish. In resistive water, it struggles to enter the fish.

3. Duty Cycle

Duty Cycle % = (Pulse Width in ms) × (Frequency in Hz) / 10. Higher duty cycles deliver more total power but increase the risk of muscular hemorrhaging or spinal injury.

4. C.P.U.E.

Catch Per Unit Effort = (Total Fish Caught) / (Total Shock Seconds). A standard metric used to estimate relative population abundance across different reaches.
