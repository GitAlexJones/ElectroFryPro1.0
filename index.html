Depletion Theory & Formulas

1. Catchability Coefficient (q)

q = C / (N × E)
The proportion of the population caught by one unit of effort. Depletion surveys mathematically assume 'q' remains constant across sweeps (remaining fish do not become harder to catch).

2. Seber-Le Cren (2-Pass Method)

N = C₁² / (C₁ - C₂)
Calculates the estimated population (N) when exactly two sweeps are performed. C₁ is fish caught in pass 1. For this to work, C₁ must be strictly greater than C₂.

3. Leslie Graphical Method (3+ Passes)

Catch Per Unit Effort (CPUE) is plotted on the Y-axis against Cumulative Catch on the X-axis. Where the linear regression line crosses the X-axis (CPUE = 0), that X-intercept is the estimated total population.
